Technical Vocational Livelihood


     The TVL track is designed to equip students with technical and vocational skills that are in demand in various industries.
In the programming strand, students are likely to learn specific skills related to computer programming, software development, and information technology.


     The curriculum in a TVL programming strand typically includes subjects such as programming languages (e.g., Python, Java, C++),
web development, database management, software engineering principles, and possibly some basics of networking or system administration.


     TVL programs usually emphasize hands-on training and practical application of skills. Students may engage in projects,
coding exercises, and possibly internships or on-the-job training to gain real-world experience in programming.


     The curriculum is often designed in collaboration with industry partners to ensure that students are learning skills that are relevant
and in demand in the job market. This may include guest lectures, industry visits, or participation in industry-led projects.


     Some TVL programming strands may offer opportunities for students to earn industry-recognized certifications or credentials, which can
enhance their employability upon graduation.


     Graduates of a TVL programming strand may pursue various career pathways, including software developer, web developer, IT support specialist,
database administrator, or software tester, among others. Some students may also choose to continue their education in college or vocational schools to further specialize in
programming or related fields.


     Overall, the TVL programming strand in senior high school provides students with a solid foundation in programming and information technology,
preparing them for entry-level positions in the tech industry or for further education and training in related fields.
